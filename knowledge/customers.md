## 2026-03-27 — Michael (setupclaw) — Sales discovery call

**Context:** Travis and team posed as buyers from "Everest Labs" to understand Michael's offering and process. He charges $5K-6K for OpenClaw setup + consulting.

**Key insights:**
- Self-describes as "forward deployed OpenClaw engineer" — that's the framing that works for enterprise
- "Automations are just text files" — our guide output format (markdown) matches what the market already uses
- "The value is knowledge of what's possible" — not the technical build itself. He's a consultant, not a developer.
- Even technical people take ~12 hours to get OpenClaw properly set up without help
- Security is his premium differentiator — "opinionated setup focused on enterprise security"
- Doesn't recommend self-hosted models — performance loss, maintenance burden, not worth it for 99% of businesses
- Complexity comes from enterprise permissioning (who has access to what), not the automations themselves
- Revenue model: setup fee ($5-6K) + ongoing Slack support + on-demand calls
- Onboarding larger orgs costs more because of permission mapping and stakeholder alignment

**What this changes:**
- Validates our core thesis: the value is in the knowledge/configuration, not in hosting
- $5K-6K is our ceiling reference for what one setup is worth
- Our output format (markdown prompts) is correct — "automations are just text files"
- Security positioning is a real differentiator we should consider
- Enterprise permissioning is a pain point we haven't addressed yet
