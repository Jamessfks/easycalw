# Product Vision

## The Core Insight

From the Michael call (2026-03-27): "The knowledge is not in the build itself — the automations are just text files. It's the knowledge of the possibilities."

Michael charges $5K as a "forward deployed OpenClaw engineer." His value isn't technical — it's knowing what's possible, how to structure it, and what to avoid. He's a consultant who happens to touch a keyboard.

**EasyClaw automates what Michael does.** Voice interview replaces the discovery call. Setup guide replaces the consultant's deliverable. We capture the same knowledge of possibilities and deliver it at a fraction of the cost.

## What We're Selling

We are not selling:
- Hosting (commoditized, $4-15/mo, 24+ providers)
- A chatbot builder (Lindy, Voiceflow, etc.)
- A no-code platform (MindStudio, Relevance AI)

We are selling:
- **Opinionated configuration intelligence** — we make the decisions customers can't make for themselves
- **Automated discovery** — the voice interview captures what a $5K consultant captures
- **Personalized setup guides** — not templates, not generic docs, but guides tailored to THIS customer's business

As Travis put it: "We are taking all the information out there and creating opinionated structures. We're making a lot of decisions for customers that customers don't have expertise for."

## The Three Layers

Our market maps to three layers. We're building Layer 2, with a path to Layer 3.

**Layer 1 — Setup (commoditized):** Deploy OpenClaw to the cloud. 24+ providers already do this. Race to bottom at $4/mo. Not our fight.

**Layer 2 — Upgrade (where we play):** Setup + configuration intelligence. Help the customer figure out what the agent should do, how to structure prompts, which skills to use, how to connect data sources. The $200-$2K range is nearly empty. We're the only company automating the discovery step.

**Layer 3 — Observability (future):** Ongoing monitoring and maintenance. "We helped you set up your agent, now we'll keep it running." $10.7B market by 2033. Zero SMB products exist. Natural expansion path.

## The Moat

What's defensible (in order of importance):
1. **Interview quality** — system prompt engineering across dozens of iterations. Hard to replicate without the same iteration cycle.
2. **Domain knowledge** — 435 skills, 72 industry files, 11 verticals. Months to build from scratch per vertical.
3. **Data flywheel** — every completed interview makes the next one better. Transcripts reveal patterns, edge cases, and customer language.
4. **Integrated pipeline** — voice (Vapi) + agent framework (OpenClaw) + guide generation in one flow. No single piece is a moat, but the combination is hard to replicate quickly.

What's NOT a moat:
- Voice-first UX (any competitor can add Vapi)
- The product concept itself (reproducible)
- Hosting (commodity)

**Speed is the primary strategic lever.** The technology is reproducible. The moat is accumulated data and quality. Every month of head start compounds.

## Infrastructure Model

Customer manages their own infrastructure:
- Pick a hosting provider (Hostinger, etc.) or use their own hardware
- Create accounts on Composio or other integration platforms
- Connect their data sources (Notion, Slack, CRM, etc.)

We provide:
- The voice interview to capture their needs
- Personalized setup guide with copy-pasteable prompts
- Opinionated configuration (which skills, which integrations, how to structure)
- The trust that the prompts are good
