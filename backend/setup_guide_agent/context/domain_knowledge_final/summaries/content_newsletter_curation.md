### 59. Automated Newsletter Curation

- **Industry:** Content Creation / Publishing
- **Target Persona:** Newsletter writers and editors who dedicate significant time each week to reading and filtering through numerous articles in order to find the most valuable content for their audience. These individuals often struggle with time constraints and seek efficient ways to streamline content curation without sacrificing quality. They typically have moderate technical skills and are comfortable integrating AI tools into their existing workflows.
- **The Execution Story:** The newsletter author configures OpenClaw to monitor around twenty carefully selected RSS feeds relevant to their industry niche. The AI agent continuously scans incoming articles, evaluating them based on engagement metrics such as social shares, comments, and relevance keywords. Each week, the agent automatically selects the top five articles that meet these criteria and generates concise, insightful summaries for each piece. These summaries are then compiled into a draft newsletter, which the author reviews and personalises with their own commentary and insights before sending. This process reduces the curation time from several hours to approximately five minutes per week, allowing the author to focus more on adding unique value and engaging with their readers.
- **Tools & Integrations Mentioned:** OpenClaw AI agent, RSS feed reader (e.g., Feedly or Inoreader), Email provider (e.g., Mailchimp or Substack)
- **Setup Notes & Warnings:** Intermediate level setup requiring some familiarity with RSS feed management and AI prompt tuning. The most challenging aspect is fine-tuning the AI’s selection criteria to consistently surface high-quality and relevant articles without overwhelming the newsletter with generic content. A key warning is that fully automating curation risks producing a newsletter that lacks personality and distinctive voice, so the human author must still review and personalise the content to maintain authenticity and reader engagement. Additionally, users should be mindful of potential subscription costs for premium RSS readers or email marketing platforms, and ensure compliance with copyright and content usage rights when sharing article summaries.
