### 30. Pet Sitter Intake and Scheduling

- **Industry:** Pet Care Services
- **Target Persona:** This skill is designed for dog walkers and pet sitters who manage multiple clients and require a streamlined way to collect comprehensive health, behavioural, and feeding information from new pet owners. These users typically prefer a conversational intake process that feels personal and engaging but may lack advanced technical skills, so they need an intuitive system that automates form creation without complicated setup.
- **The Execution Story:** When a new client contacts the pet sitting business via text message, the OpenClaw agent initiates a friendly, guided conversation to gather essential details about the pet, including vaccination history, feeding routines, medication allergies, and behavioural notes. As the client responds, the agent dynamically organises the information and uses a PDF generation tool to compile a professional, easy-to-read intake form. This PDF is then delivered to the pet sitter’s records system, ensuring they have accurate, up-to-date information before each visit. This workflow reduces manual data entry, minimises errors, and saves several hours per week by automating client onboarding. The conversational format also improves client experience by making the intake feel less like paperwork and more like a personalised chat.
- **Tools & Integrations Mentioned:** OpenClaw AI platform, SMS/text messaging interface for client communication, PDF generation software for creating formatted intake documents
- **Setup Notes & Warnings:** The setup complexity is intermediate, requiring some familiarity with OpenClaw’s skill configuration and integration with messaging and PDF tools. The most challenging aspect is ensuring the AI accurately captures and retains critical health information, such as medication allergies or special care instructions, without hallucinations or omissions. It is essential to implement a verification step where the sitter reviews the raw chat transcripts against the final PDF to prevent potentially dangerous errors. Additionally, handling sensitive pet health data demands careful attention to data privacy and security practices to protect client information.
