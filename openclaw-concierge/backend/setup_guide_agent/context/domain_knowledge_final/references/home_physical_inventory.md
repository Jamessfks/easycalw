Jesse Genet is a homeschooling parent of four who has five OpenClaw agents running on a stack of Mac Minis on her desk. She uses these tools to solve real, everyday problems, especially as a parent.

## Workflow 1: Automating Homeschool with an AI Second Brain

Jesse was using Obsidian to build a "second brain" for her family's homeschool curriculum but didn't have time for meticulous data entry. She layered an AI agent (Sylvie) on top of her Obsidian vault.

### Digitizing Curriculum from Photos
She took pictures of entire curriculum books (e.g., "Teach Your Child to Read in 100 Easy Lessons", BFSU science curriculum). The agent ingested and understood the full context — not just OCR but understanding structure, concepts, and lesson progressions.

### Generating Lesson Plans and Creative Materials
Sylvie generates structured lesson plans from each chapter — extracting key objectives, vocabulary, and materials lists. She also generates watercolor illustrations for concepts using Google Gemini, guided by Sylvie's persona defined in soul.md.

## Workflow 2: Building a Custom "Slop-Free" Kids' TV App

Frustrated with low-quality "AI slop" on YouTube, Jesse used her coding agent Cole to build "Mira" — a custom app pulling from curated YouTube channels to create themed streams of high-quality content. Minimal UI: just a "Go" button, forward/back, and pause. No comments, no suggested videos.

Built over four days, mostly in small pockets of time from her phone. When Cole said deploying to TV wasn't possible, Jesse pushed back: "Try harder, Cole." Cole found a solution via Google TV streamer.

## Workflow 3: Bridging the Digital-Physical Divide with a Photo Inventory

Jesse owned educational toys, books, and supplies that would sit forgotten in cupboards. Her agent couldn't help because it didn't know what physical items existed.

### Photographing and Cataloging
She took photos of everything — every educational toy, book, and Montessori material. Sent photos to Sylvie: "I wanna make an inventory of my learning supplies. Here's the photos."

Sylvie created a structured inventory in Obsidian — identifying each item, its type, appropriate age range, and description. A photo of a wooden board became "Montessori language materials, Wooden alphabet tracing board, age range 3-5."

### Connecting Inventory to Lessons
Because inventory and lesson plans live in the same system, Sylvie can suggest relevant physical items during lesson planning. Plus, Jesse gave Sylvie printer access — she can take a photo and say "Sylvie, print this" for instant worksheet printing.

## Jesse's Philosophy: Managing Agents Like Employees

- **Physical Partitioning for Security**: Each of five agents runs on a separate Mac Mini. Finance agent (Finn) has read-only bank access but no external communication. Scheduling agent (Claire) can use iMessage but has zero financial access.
- **Progressive Trust**: Agents get limited access first, expanded as proven trustworthy. No agent gets personal email — they get their own email addresses.
- **Specialized Roles and Personas**: Each agent has a name and specific job defined in soul.md.
- **The "Decision File"**: A decision.md file in Obsidian logs final decisions so agents won't re-litigate settled topics.
